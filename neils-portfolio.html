        <p>The architecture of the DCF model follows a sequence of six analytical steps:</p>

        <ol className="list-decimal pl-5 space-y-2 text-slate-300">

          <li><strong>Forecast UFCF:</strong> Forecast the Unlevered Free Cash Flow over an explicit projection period (typically 5-10 years).</li>

          <li><strong>Discount Cash Flows:</strong> Discount both the forecasted UFCFs and the Terminal Value back to the present day using the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) to find Enterprise Value.</li>

          <li><strong>Add Non-Operating Assets:</strong> Add excess cash to the Enterprise Value.</li>

          <li><strong>Subtract Non-Equity Claims:</strong> Subtract total debt, capital leases, and preferred stock to arrive at Equity Value.</li>

          <li><strong>Calculate Value Per Share:</strong> Divide the aggregate Equity Value by the fully diluted number of shares outstanding to arrive at the intrinsic value per share.</li>
